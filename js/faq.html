Frequently asked questions

  • What is a PDEx score?
    • A precentile measure of the strength of association between sample set and CMap perturbagen compared to an empircal background. Range is -100 to 100. Scores > |90| beat out 90% of background connections and are considered strong.
  • How do I interpret a heatmap on the gene page?
    • Columns represent different sample sets from alterations in the given gene. Rows represent CMap perturbagens. The value in a cell is the PDEx score for association between the sample set and the CMap pert. The default scheme colors all strong connections (> |90|). A tutorial on the features of the morpheus heatmap viewer is available here.
  • What are the different pert_types?
    • trt_sh.cgs: consensus signature of gene knock-down with multiple shRNA sequences
    • trt_oe: synthetic ORF overexpression
    • trt_cp: compound treatment
    • PCL: Pharmacologic class: aggregate signature of many compounds targeting the same pathway
