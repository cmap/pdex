About PDEx

Compare patient-derived gene expression profiles with perturbational signatures in the Connectivity Map (CMap)

PDEx tests for associations between a feature present in a subset of samples (gene mutation, tumor receptor status, patient drug response, etc) and perturbations profiled in CMap (drug treatments, gene knock-down, gene overexpression). A high positive PDEx score indicates samples with the feature of interest and the perturbation induced similar gene expression changes. A high negative score indicates opposite changes - genes upregulated in one are downregulated in the other. Intermediate scores indicate no detectable association between the two

Examine associations with common alterations in The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA)

Example: TP53 mutations in TCGA

Tumor Protein 53 (TP53) is a tumor suppressor gene that is frequently inactivated by somatic missense mutation in cancer. TP53 mutations occur in 1931 out of 10147 TCGA samples (19%) across different cancers. How does TP53 mutation in TCGA compare to TP53 knock-down in CMap?

Example: Connections to KEAP1 and NRF2 (NFE2L2) signalling

KEAP1 functions to keep NRF2 in the cytoplasm and regulates the antioxidant response signalling of NRF2. Upon inactivation of KEAP1 by mutations, we expect to see a signature similar to NRF2 overexpression. This is true for KEAP1 mutations, NRF2 mutations, and NRF2 copy number gains!
